Milwaukee resources

Local crisis lines, peer support, and community organizations across Greater Milwaukee. A starting point — these are independent organizations we don't run.

Crisis & helplines

  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline — call or text 988 (24/7).
  • 911 — medical or safety emergencies.
  • Milwaukee County 24-Hour Crisis Line — (414) 257-7222 (all ages, 24/7; can dispatch Mobile Crisis).
  • IMPACT 211 — call 211, or text your ZIP to 898211 (24/7 connection to local help).
  • Full crisis details are on our Get Help Now page.

Featured resource

A free, local source of mental-health knowledge we think is worth your time.

Dr. John Weaver & the Healthy Thinking Center

Dr. John Weaver, Psy.D., has decades of experience and is a wealth of knowledge in the mental-health field. His Stress Management & Mental Health Clinics offer medication management for teens through adulthood, psychotherapy, and an innovative Healthy Thinking Center. He also shares free, plain-language mental-health videos anyone can learn from on YouTube.

Peer support & warmlines

Warmlines are free, confidential peer-support lines for when you're not in crisis but could use someone to talk to. For a crisis, call 988 or (414) 257-7222.

Warmline (Milwaukee)

Peer counselors offer non-crisis support. Call 414-777-4729. Calls are limited to ~15 minutes so everyone gets a turn.

Parachute House

Milwaukee peer-run respite — phone peer support for adults under increased stress. Call 414-877-5918 (you may leave a message for a call-back).

Hopeline (text)

Text-based peer support, 24/7 — text HOPELINE to 741741.

NAMI HelpLine

Information, resources, and support (not a crisis line). Call 1-800-950-6264 or text "NAMI" to 62640.

Support groups & community organizations

NAMI Southeast Wisconsin

Free support groups, education, and family programs across Milwaukee, Waukesha & Jefferson counties. Office (262) 524-8886. Check the site for current group schedules.

IMPACT 211

Call 211 for connections to counseling, support groups, food, housing, and community resources across Greater Milwaukee.

Milwaukee County Behavioral Health

County crisis services, Crisis Resource Centers, and Access Clinics for mental-health and substance-use support.

Counseling & lower-cost care

Jewish Family Services

Counseling for people of all faiths; free/low-cost sessions with supervised trainees for qualifying uninsured/underinsured clients.

16th Street Community Health Centers

Community health centers offering behavioral-health services on a sliding scale for underserved and uninsured residents.

UWM Psychology Clinic

University training clinic offering therapy on an income-based sliding scale. Confirm current details.

Wisconsin DHS — Mental Health

State hub with a county-by-county provider directory and program information.

Community & identity-specific support

Diverse & Resilient

LGBTQ+ health nonprofit serving Wisconsin — peer mental-health support groups and resources. Call/text 414-856-5428.

Milwaukee LGBT Community Center

Counseling and support-group referrals plus a community network for LGBTQ+ residents.

Mental Health America of Wisconsin

Statewide MHA — runs the Milwaukee Warmline and a searchable resource directory.

Grief, youth & family

Children's Wisconsin — Grief Support

Free grief support groups for kids and teens (5–18) and a young-adult group; email griefsupport@childrenswi.org.

Healing Hearts of SE Wisconsin

Free peer grief support for children, youth, and families coping with many kinds of loss.

Wraparound Milwaukee

Milwaukee County care coordination for children and youth with complex mental-health needs.
