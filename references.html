References & sources

The educational content on this site is drawn from authoritative public-health and professional sources. We summarize in plain language and link back to the originals. This page supports the clinical review of our content.

This is a starting reference list for our reviewers and for anyone who wants to read further — it is not exhaustive, and external sites are independent of MKE Mental Health.

Crisis & safety

  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
  • SAMHSA — 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (incl. Veterans "press 1" / Spanish "press 2")
  • Crisis Text Line · Veterans Crisis Line · The Trevor Project
  • SAMHSA National Helpline (1-800-662-HELP) · SAMHSA Safety Plan
  • NIMH — Suicide Prevention & Warning Signs

Milwaukee & Wisconsin resources

  • Milwaukee County Behavioral Health Services (Crisis Line, Mobile Crisis, Crisis Resource Centers, Access Clinics)
  • IMPACT 211 (call 211)
  • NAMI Southeast Wisconsin · NAMI Wisconsin — Talk to Someone (warmlines)
  • Wisconsin DHS — 988

Therapy & treatment

  • NIMH — Psychotherapies
  • APA — CBT, EMDR, Understanding Psychotherapy, Choosing a Therapist
  • SAMHSA FindTreatment.gov

Conditions & topics

  • NIMH — Anxiety, Depression
  • NAMI — Mental Health Conditions
  • APA — Grief, Stress & the body
  • Mayo Clinic — Burnout, Insomnia · CDC — About Sleep

Coping & everyday well-being

  • Mayo Clinic — Relaxation techniques (paced breathing)
  • Univ. of Rochester — 5-4-3-2-1 grounding
  • CDC — Activity & brain health, Social connection, Managing stress
  • APA — Expressive writing, Media overload ("doomscrolling") · NIAAA (alcohol & mental health)

Insurance & paying for care

  • HealthCare.gov Glossary (premium, deductible, copay, coinsurance, out-of-pocket max, in/out-of-network, prior authorization)
  • CMS — Mental Health Parity (MHPAEA) · APA — Parity guide
  • SAMHSA — Free & low-cost treatment · NAMI — Affording treatment (sliding scale, superbills, EAPs)
